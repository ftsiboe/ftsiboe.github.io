A core part of my work is **translating rigorous analysis into
decision-ready products** for farmers, agribusiness, and policymakers,
and engaging directly with the people who use it. My outreach spans
policy briefings, stakeholder-facing publications, podcasts and panels,
and reusable software, alongside earlier extension experience built
around farm and agribusiness fieldwork.

## Policy briefings and interagency engagement

While at the USDA Economic Research Service, I delivered briefings on
USDA-ERS reports to the **Under Secretary for Farm Production and
Conservation**, the Farm Production and Conservation Business Center
(FPAC), the Office of Management and Budget (OMB), and the Office of the
Chief Economist (OCE). I presented on the size, scope, and market
penetration of the FCIP to an audience of **142 participants at the 2025
Agricultural Outlook Forum**, USDA’s oldest and largest annual
stakeholder gathering, and I continue to leverage connections across
USDA-ERS, RMA, NASS, FPAC, and OCE to strengthen institutional
partnerships for risk-management programs.

## Briefs, podcasts, and panels

I lead and co-author **ARPC policy briefs** that turn technical findings
into concise, accessible formats on prevented-planting buy-up
elimination, supplemental crop-insurance risk reduction, premium
interest deferrals, regional financial stress, and the evolution of U.S.
crop-insurance plans. To extend the reach of this research I have:

- Appeared on the **Morning Coffee and Ag Markets** podcast (Episode 77)
  on how insured farmers can adjust to USDA’s EARP rule;
- Served on a panel at the **2025 Midwest Extension Farm Management and
  Outlook Conference** in Omaha, NE; and
- Contributed scheduled **University of Arkansas Extension** and
  **Southern Ag Today** articles on rice.

Browse the [risk-management
publications](https://ftsiboe.github.io/publications/risk-management/)
for the underlying briefs and reports.

## Tools that outlast a single project

I view well-documented, reusable software as a form of outreach: it lets
others reproduce and build on the analysis. I lead or contribute to
open-source R packages (for FCIP simulation, price and basis,
production-cost calibration, agricultural peril metrics, and editorial
workflows) that are deployed across the Center’s publications and now
form part of its analytical backbone. Explore my
[software](https://ftsiboe.github.io/r-packages/).
